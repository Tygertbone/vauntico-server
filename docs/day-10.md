## üéØ What Is a Hook?

**Hook = The first 1-3 seconds of your video that makes people STOP scrolling**

**Bad hook example:**
"Hey guys, welcome back to my channel, today I'm going to show you..."

**Result:** Scrolled past in 0.5 seconds.

**Good hook example:**
"I lost 20kg using ONLY my phone. Here's how..."

**Result:** Viewers STOP. They WATCH. Algorithm pushes video.

---

## üß† The Psychology of Hooks

Hooks work because they trigger one of these emotions:

1. **Curiosity** ("The #1 mistake keeping you broke...")
2. **Shock** ("I made R5,000 in 3 days. Here's proof...")
3. **Relatability** ("If you're tired of being broke, watch this...")
4. **Urgency** ("Do this before February ends or lose money...")
5. **Controversy** ("Everyone says save money. I say spend more...")
6. **Promise** ("3 steps to gain 1,000 followers this week...")

**Every viral video uses at least ONE of these emotions in the first 3 seconds.**

## üìä Hook Testing Strategy

Film the SAME video with 3 DIFFERENT hooks. Post them. See which performs best.

**Example: Fitness video about home workouts**

**Hook A (Curiosity):** "The #1 mistake keeping you out of shape..."  
**Hook B (Promise):** "3 exercises to get fit at home (no gym)..."  
**Hook C (Relatability):** "If you're tired of expensive gyms, watch this..."

**Post all 3. Check after 24 hours:**
- Hook A: 230 views
- Hook B: 1,400 views ‚úÖ **WINNER**
- Hook C: 180 views

**Lesson learned:** Your audience responds best to PROMISE hooks. Use more of those!
