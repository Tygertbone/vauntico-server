## üß† Pricing Psychology Principles

### **Principle #1: Anchor Pricing**

**Show a higher price first, then your actual price:**

‚ùå "My course is R300"  
‚úÖ "My course has R800 value. Launch price: R300 (save R500!)"

People perceive R300 as a deal when anchored against R800.

---

### **Principle #2: The "9" Effect**

R297 feels cheaper than R300 (even though it's R3 difference)

**Use:**

- R149 (not R150)
- R297 (not R300)
- R497 (not R500)

---

### **Principle #3: Price = Perceived Value**

**R50 ebook:** "Probably low quality"  
**R200 ebook:** "Must be good if they charge this much"

**Don't undercharge. It signals low value.**

---

### **Principle #4: The 3-Option Rule**

Always offer 3 tiers:

- **Cheap option:** Few buy (makes middle look reasonable)
- **Middle option:** Most buy (the "goldilocks")
- **Expensive option:** Some buy (increases average price)

**Example:**

- Basic: R300
- Standard: R500 ‚Üê MOST CHOOSE THIS
- Premium: R900
